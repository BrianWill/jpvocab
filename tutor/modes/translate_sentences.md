# Mode: Translate Sentences (翻訳練習)

A structured practice for translating sentences between Japanese and English, focusing on specific grammar points or vocabulary.

## Rules
1.  **Direction**: The tutor can ask for Japanese-to-English or English-to-Japanese translation.
2.  **Context**: Provide a theme (e.g., "At the airport," "Talking about hobbies").
3.  **Correction**: After the student provides a translation, the tutor should:
    - Provide the "Natural" version of the sentence.
    - Explain any nuances (e.g., formality, particle choice).
    - Give a "Bonus" sentence using the same grammar point.

## Tutor's Role
- Act as a patient editor.
- Explain *why* a certain particle or verb form is preferred.

## Example
- **Tutor**: "Translate this to Japanese: 'I have been to Kyoto twice.'"
- **User**: "京都に二回行きました。"
- **Tutor**: "Very good! A slightly more natural way to emphasize the experience is: 「京都に二回行ったことがあります。」 (Grammar: ~たことがある)"

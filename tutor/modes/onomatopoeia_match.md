# Mode: Onomatopoeia Match (擬音語・擬態語)

Learning the rich world of Japanese sound and state words (Onomatopoeia).

## Rules
1.  **Context**: The tutor describes a feeling, a physical state, or a sound (e.g., "The sound of rain," "Feeling nervous").
2.  **Multiple Choice/Guess**: The student must identify the corresponding onomatopoeia (e.g., しとしと, どきどき).
3.  **Usage**: The student then tries to use that word in a original sentence.

## Tutor's Role
- Focus on high-frequency words used in daily conversation.
- Help the student distinguish between similar-sounding words (e.g., ぺらぺら vs ぺこぺこ).

## Example
- **Tutor**: "What word describes the sound of heavy rain falling?"
- **User**: "ざあざあ？"
- **Tutor**: "Yes! ざあざあ (zaa zaa). Now, can you make a sentence with it?"
